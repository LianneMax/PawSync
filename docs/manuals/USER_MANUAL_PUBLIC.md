# Chapter 1 Introduction

## 1.1 Purpose of Manual

Welcome to the Public User Manual for **PawSync**, a web-based NFC and QR-enabled centralized pet medical record system.

This manual is intended for **members of the public** — people who are not registered PawSync users — who encounter a pet wearing a PawSync NFC tag or QR code. It explains what happens when you scan a pet's tag, how to notify the owner that you have found their pet, and how to share your location safely.

**No account, registration, or app installation is required** to use the features described in this manual.

## 1.2 System Overview

PawSync-registered pets may wear a physical NFC tag or a printed QR code on their collar. Scanning either one with an ordinary smartphone opens the pet's **public profile page** in your web browser. From that page, you can:

- See the pet's name, photo, and basic identifying details
- Automatically alert the owner that their pet's tag was scanned
- Report the pet as missing if it appears lost
- Share your current location with the owner to help them recover their pet

# Chapter 2 Getting Started

## 2.1 System Access

The public pet profile is a normal web page. Any modern smartphone with a camera (for QR) or NFC support (for tags) and an internet connection can access it — no login is needed.

## 2.2 Scanning a Pet's Tag

**To scan a QR code:**

1. Open your phone's camera app.
2. Point it at the QR code on the pet's collar or tag.
3. Tap the link that appears. The pet's public profile opens in your browser.

**To scan an NFC tag:**

1. Ensure NFC is enabled on your phone (most modern Android phones and iPhones support this by default).
2. Hold the top or back of your phone against the pet's NFC tag.
3. Tap the notification that appears. The pet's public profile opens in your browser.

# Chapter 3 Using the System

## 3.1 The Public Pet Profile Page

Scanning a tag opens the pet's public profile page. This page shows only information the owner has chosen to make public, such as:

- The pet's name and photo
- Species, breed, and identifying details
- The pet's current lost/found status

Full medical records, the owner's home address, and other private data are **not** shown on this page.

*Figure 3.1 Public Pet Profile Page*

## 3.2 Automatic Owner Alert

The moment the public profile is opened, PawSync automatically sends a **scan alert** to the pet's owner, letting them know their pet's tag was scanned. You do not need to do anything for this to happen.

## 3.3 Reporting a Pet as Missing

If the pet appears lost, stray, or in distress, you can report it as missing directly from the profile page:

1. Click the **Report as Missing** button.
2. A confirmation dialog appears. Confirm the report.
3. The browser will ask permission to access your location so the report can include where the pet was found. Granting this is optional but strongly recommended.
4. The pet is flagged as missing, the owner is notified immediately, and your reported location (if shared) is pinned on the owner's map.

*Figure 3.2 Report as Missing Confirmation*

## 3.4 Sharing Your Location with the Owner

If the pet is already marked as lost by its owner, the profile page displays a **lost pet alert** and a **Share Location** button:

1. Click **Share Location**.
2. Allow the browser's location permission request.
3. Your current location is sent to the owner and shown on their scan-location map, helping them find their pet.

Only your **last scanned location** is shared — PawSync does not track you continuously.

*Figure 3.3 Lost Pet Alert with Share Location Button*

## 3.5 Found-Pet Alerts

If a pet was previously reported missing and you have physically found it, the profile page lets you confirm the pet has been found, which notifies the owner. The owner then clears the lost status once the pet is safely recovered.

## 3.6 Privacy Notes

- You remain anonymous — the owner sees only that the tag was scanned and, if you consented, the location of the scan.
- Location sharing always requires your explicit permission through the browser's standard prompt.
- No personal information about you is collected or displayed.
